Sensory Processing

Lesson Overview

In this lesson, you will learn about sensory processing and develop strategies to manage sensory sensitivities. Understanding your sensory needs can help you navigate daily life more comfortably and confidently.

Key Points

  • Understanding the different sensory systems and how they interact
  • Recognizing your personal sensory preferences and triggers
  • Developing coping strategies for overwhelming sensory experiences
  • Creating a sensory-friendly environment at home and work/school
  • Communicating your sensory needs to others effectively

Practice Exercise: Sensory Inventory

Complete this sensory inventory to better understand your sensory preferences:

  1. Visual: List 3 visual inputs that you find calming and 3 that you find overwhelming.
  2. Auditory: Identify 3 sounds you enjoy and 3 sounds that bother you.
  3. Tactile: Describe 3 textures you like touching and 3 you avoid.
  4. Taste/Smell: Name 3 flavors or scents you love and 3 you dislike.
  5. Movement: List 3 types of movement that make you feel good and 3 that make you uncomfortable.

After completing the inventory, think about strategies you can use to increase positive sensory experiences and minimize negative ones in your daily life.
